Josh's salty white sauce

White sauce is considered to be one of the "mother sauces" in french cooking.
They might call it something fancy like bechamel but iunno. You can use it as a gravy,
as a stew base, in a lasagna, wherever! It's extremely versatile.

The important thing to remember is that while the amount of milk may change,
The flour and rice always remain at a 1:1 ratio! Here we go.

Ingredients:

  • Milk (whole milk works best!)
  • Butter(margarine or other oils may do in a pinch, but are not reliable
  • White flour

Steps:

  1. In a frying pan on medium heat, melt one tablespoon of butter.
  2. Add one teaspoon of white flour and thoroughly mix with butter until a sort of paste is created. This is called a roux.
  3. Allow the roux to cook in the pan for perhaps two minutes. It should bubble and spread, and slightly darken in color.
    Don't alow it to get too dark, too fast! If your roux burns, start over from the beginning.
    Note: You are likely to burn your roux a few times until you learn to do it well consistently.
    If you're never burned a roux, you've never made a roux.
  4. Pour in 2 cups of milk and mix thoroughly with roux. Allow the mixture to cook and thicken.
  5. Continue gradually adding milk and cooking. The mixture will keep thickening after it's brought
    to boiling again. Once you have reached the desired thickness, season the sauce with salt and pepper.
    Other spices can be used as you please

And that's it! I hope you enjoy my delicious white sauce. ( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°)
